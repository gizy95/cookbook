Baklava

Baklava is a dessert from Turkey and means “puff pastry pie” in German. With our simple recipe you can easily make original baklava yourself.

Ingridiens

  • Total Time: 45 mins
  • 125g Filo dough from the refrigerated shelf or 5 sheets of strudel dough
  • 50g Honey
  • 70ml Water
  • 150g Sugar
  • 50g chopped pistachios
  • 120g chopped almonds
  • 120g chopped walnuts
  • 1 teaspoon Cinnamon
  • 125g liquid butter
  • 3 tbsp chopped pistachios for garnishsome butter for the mold

Steps

  • 1. Brush the baking dish (20cm x 25cm) with melted butter. Finely chop all the nuts together with the cinnamon (if you have one, you can use an electric mixer). Preheat the oven to 170 degrees top/bottom heat .
  • 2. Cut the dough sheets into the correct shape and spread the first dough sheet with melted butter and place it in the shape. Spread 2 tablespoons of the nut mixture evenly on top, repeat the process until the ingredients are used up, finish with a sheet of dough.
  • 3. Cut the baklava into small squares or diamonds. Then put water, sugar and honey in a pot and bring to the boil. Cook the mixture until it becomes a syrup (about 3-4 minutes). Then remove the pot from the heat.
  • 4. Pour the hot syrup evenly over the cut pieces in the baking dish. Then bake the baklava in the preheated oven for approx. 35 minutes . The baklava is ready when the dough sheets are golden brown. Garnish each piece with some chopped pistachios.
  • 5. Baklava has a particularly long shelf life thanks to honey. You can store the dessert at room temperature for about 2 weeks. If you want to keep the specialty for longer, you can simply freeze it and keep it that way for up to 6 months.
